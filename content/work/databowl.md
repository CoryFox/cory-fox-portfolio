## Context

Databowl is a SaaS platform for lead validation, cleaning, and management. When I joined in January 2018, it was internally nicknamed the "Grey Beast" because the UI felt dated, inconsistent, and difficult to move through.

## Key changes

### Reframed onboarding around process

- Redesigned onboarding as structured wizard-style journeys that matched how setup tasks actually needed to happen.
- Made first-campaign setup clearer by guiding users through key decisions in the right order.

### Reduced friction in high-impact configuration

- Reworked validation rule setup, deduplication controls, and export integration flows.
- Removed ambiguity from the areas most likely to affect lead quality and downstream outcomes.

### Improved design maturity

- Produced journey maps, interactive prototypes, and full developer handoff packages.
- Evolved tooling from UXPin and Axure RP into Figma with more structured component standards.

### Kept product direction grounded

- Worked closely with leadership and engineering to keep UX direction practical and buildable.
- Helped turn a complex internal tool into a clearer product experience that could scale with the client base.

## Outcomes

- Reduced onboarding churn by improving setup clarity and flow completion.
- Improved confidence in early platform use for agency and enterprise teams.
- Strengthened Databowl's product-market credibility by turning a legacy system into a more mature SaaS experience.

Precise commercial figures are excluded for confidentiality, so this case emphasises the product and workflow improvements that drove the change.
